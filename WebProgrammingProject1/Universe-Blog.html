The universe stretches over 93 billion light-years, and its wonders range from the formation of stars and planets to the magnificent galaxies that span the cosmos.

One of the most intriguing aspects of the universe is its continuously expanding nature. The fabric of space itself is stretching, pushing galaxies farther apart with every passing second. But the universe is not only a vast space of galaxies and stars. It also holds countless unexplored mysteries, like dark matter, dark energy, and black holes, all of which shape the universe in unseen ways.

Interesting Facts:

  • The universe is approximately 13.8 billion years old.
  • It is expanding at an accelerated rate due to dark energy.
  • There are an estimated 100 billion galaxies in the observable universe.
