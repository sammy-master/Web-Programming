What Are Galaxies?

Galaxies are massive systems of stars, planets, gas, dust, and dark matter held together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes, including spiral, elliptical, and irregular.

Fun Fact: The Milky Way galaxy contains over 100 billion stars!

Types of Galaxies

  • Spiral Galaxies: Like our Milky Way, these have a central bulge with arms spiraling outward.
  • Elliptical Galaxies: Shaped like stretched-out spheres, they often contain older stars.
  • Irregular Galaxies: Chaotic in shape, often formed by galactic collisions.
